Diversity and evolution of cerebellar folding in mammal

Based on histological data from 56 mammalian species and developed methods to analyze cerebellar folding and molecular layer thickness. Using comparative methods, we found a stabilizing selection process governing the evolution of cerebellar and cerebral anatomy, with a coordinated increase and decrease in size and folding. Our study highlights the strong correlation between cerebral and cerebellar volumes and the disproportionately higher folding in larger cerebella. These findings offer new insights into the diversity and evolution of cerebellar folding and its impact on brain organization across species.

Authors: Katja Heuer, Nicolas Traut, Alexandra A. de Sousa, Sofie Valk, Roberto Toro
bioRxiv 2022.12.30.522292; https://doi.org/10.1101/2022.12.30.522292
